Special note about humidity:

Here we are looking at relative humidity which is the most common humidity measurement. Relative humidity is directly dependent on the temperature of the surrounding air since cold air holds less moisture than warm air. So while Ayan, Russia and Port Blair, India both have a humidity of 100%, because the temperature in Ayan was 5.5°F versus 84°F in Port Blair, the amount of water vapor in the air will be significantly lower in Ayan due to the colders temperature.

Cloudiness

Similar to humidity, cloudiness does not appear to correlate with latitude. There are a range of cloudiness percentages across all latitudes in both hemispheres.

Special note about cloudiness:

While the general mechanics of cloud formation would suggest that higher moisture in the air (i.e. closer to the equator) would result in greater cloud coverage, we can see here that is not necessarily the case. This indicates that there are other weather variables which play a role in overall cloudiness, and indeed the cloud feedback cycle is known uncertainty in climate science.

Wind Speeds

Wind speeds do not show a strong correlation with latitude although there does appear to be a weak correlation. A small dip in wind speeds can be seen near the equator with the highest wind speeds being recorded at high latitudes. However there's not enough here to draw any conclusions and more work would need to be done in order to account for isolated weather events (e.g. tropical storm moving past a weather station at the time of reading).

Thigns to consider...

  • Data is biased towards the northern hemisphere due to greater landmass and therefore more cities.
  • Converting relative humidity to specific humidity may provide better insight as that would account for the density of water in the air.
  • To obtain better wind speed analysis, other factors such as current weather events and elevation should be controlled for.
  • Time of day was not accounted for in any of the data. Measurements taken at night have the potential to be drastically different than those taken during the day. (E.g. desert regions having large temperature swings and the effect that has on localized weather)
  • All data comes from a single day. A better approach would be to analyze readings for each city on multiple days throughout the year. This would help alleviate issues of seasonality.
